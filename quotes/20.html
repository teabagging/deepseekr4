❓In a class, 7 students like to play basketball, 10 like to play cricket, 8 like to play soccer, and 2 like to play all three sports. Moreover, 5 students like to play both basketball and cricket, 4 students like to play both basketball and soccer, and 3 students like to play both cricket and soccer. Calculate how many students like to play at least one of these sports (basketball, cricket, or soccer)?

💡First, I identify the number of students who like each individual sport: Basketball (B) = 7, Cricket (C) = 10, and Soccer (S) = 8.Next, I note the overlaps between the sports:- Both Basketball and Cricket (BC) = 5- Both Basketball and Soccer (BS) = 4- Both Cricket and Soccer (CS) = 3Additionally, there are 2 students who like all three sports (BCS).To apply the inclusion-exclusion principle, I adjust the overlaps to exclude those who like all three sports:- Adjusted BC = 5 - 2 = 3- Adjusted BS = 4 - 2 = 2- Adjusted CS = 3 - 2 = 1Finally, I calculate the total number of students who like at least one sport by adding the individual sport preferences, subtracting the adjusted overlaps, and adding back the students who like all three sports:Total = B + C + S - (Adjusted BC + Adjusted BS + Adjusted CS) + BCSTotal = 7 + 10 + 8 - (3 + 2 + 1) + 2 = 21Therefore, 21 students like to play at least one of the sports.
